About Nicole

A clinically attentive practice for adults in Illinois.

Treatment begins through speaking. Patients are invited to freely express thoughts, feelings, memories, associations, and experiences as they arise, including what may feel uncertain, repetitive, difficult to articulate, or not yet fully understood. Within expression itself, one may begin to listen differently to what is being said, notice patterns that emerge over time, and encounter aspects of oneself with greater depth and clarity.

Through this unfolding process, reflection and introspection may deepen, allowing meaning to emerge in ways that are not always immediately apparent. At times, this may lead toward change—whether in one's relationship to the self, in the capacity to sustain interpersonal relationships, or in discovering healthier ways of functioning. Change is not something imposed, but something that may emerge through attention, inquiry, and a closer engagement with one's internal life.

Treatment is understood as an evolving process shaped through language, reflection, and the meanings carried within experience, emotion, and repetition. Psychoanalytic work invites a deeper engagement with oneself and offers the possibility of greater understanding over time.
